Welcome to the tiny world inside your computer

When you look at a computer, you see a screen, buttons, maybe a keyboard and a mouse. But inside… there’s a team of parts working together super fast.

Let’s meet the most important parts!

The Brain — CPU

The CPU is like the computer’s brain. It doesn’t think like people do, but it:

  • Reads instructions
  • Makes tiny decisions
  • Tells other parts what to do

Kid version:

The CPU is like the teacher in a classroom. The teacher reads the lesson, tells everyone what to do, and keeps things moving.

No teacher = chaos. No CPU = the computer does nothing.

The Memory — RAM

RAM is where the computer keeps things it is using right now.

Kid version:

RAM is like the desk in your classroom. You put your book and pencil on the desk while you’re working.

When you clear the desk, those things are gone. That’s what happens when you turn off a computer — RAM gets wiped clean.

The Storage — Hard Drive / Disk

Storage is where the computer keeps things for a long time, like:

  • Games
  • Pictures
  • Videos
  • Homework
  • Apps

Kid version:

Storage is like your school bag or toy box. You put things in, they stay there overnight, and you can take them out later.

If you delete something, it’s like throwing it away.

The Screen — The Face

The screen shows you what the computer is doing.

Kid version:

The screen is like the computer’s face. It shows pictures, words, games, and videos.

If the computer is working, you see things. If something is broken, you might see nothing at all.

The Keyboard and Mouse — The Hands and Ears

These help the computer understand you.

Kid version:

They are like the computer’s ears (it listens to you) and its hands (it feels your clicks and taps).

You press, you click, you type… and the computer listens.

Putting it all together

Let’s imagine you click: “Play Game”.

What happens inside?

  1. The keyboard/mouse hears you
  2. The brain (CPU) reads the rules
  3. The memory (RAM) holds what you’re using right now
  4. The storage finds the game
  5. The screen shows you the game

All of this happens faster than you can blink.

Try this: “Be the computer” (again!)

You need:

  • One “Brain”
  • One “Desk”
  • One “Toy Box”
  • One “Face”

Make labels on paper: Brain = CPU, Desk = RAM, Toy Box = Storage, Face = Screen.

Act out a simple moment like “Play Game” or “Open a picture”. You’ll see every part has a job.

The big idea

A computer is not one thing. It is a team.

  • Brain thinks (CPU)
  • Desk holds things you need right now (RAM)
  • Toy box stores things for later (Storage)
  • Face shows you what’s happening (Screen)
  • Hands and ears listen to you (Keyboard & Mouse)

Next time, we’ll learn how computers talk to each other using something called… the Internet.
