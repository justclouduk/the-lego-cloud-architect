What is this site?

The LEGO Cloud Architect is a simple, friendly website that helps kids understand computers, the internet, and modern technology without big scary words.

It’s mainly designed for ages 5–10, but older kids (and adults!) can enjoy it too.

What kids will learn

Kids will learn things like:

  • What a computer is (and what it is not)
  • What’s inside a computer (CPU, memory, storage)
  • How the internet works
  • What “the cloud” means
  • What AI is (in a simple, safe way)

More importantly, they’ll practice thinking skills like curiosity, problem-solving, and explaining ideas clearly.

How to use the lessons

  • Keep lessons short (5–10 minutes is plenty)
  • Read together if you can
  • Ask your child to explain it back to you
  • Try the little “missions” and activities
  • Let them ask questions (even the weird ones!)

Screen time (the sensible bit)

These lessons are designed to be short and calm. The goal isn’t endless scrolling — it’s learning, talking, and then going off to do something in the real world.

Many lessons include simple offline activities you can do with paper, toys, or just imagination.

Safety and values

This site is built to be kid-friendly and low-stress.

  • No comments and no chat
  • No ads aimed at children
  • Simple pages that load quickly
  • Content written to be encouraging and age-appropriate

For teachers

If you’re a teacher, welcome! Over time I plan to add simple classroom ideas, printable activities, and small challenges that work well for groups.

About the builder

I’m a UK-based IT architect and a dad of two. I built this site because my kids ask brilliant questions about technology — and I wanted answers that are fun, simple, and actually make sense.

Support and helping schools

In the future, I’d love this project to help fund resources for schools. For now, the focus is building great lessons and making them freely available.
