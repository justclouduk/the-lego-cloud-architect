So… what is the internet?

The internet is a way for computers all over the world to talk to each other.

When you watch a video, send a message, or open a website, your computer is talking to another computer far away.

The big idea

The internet is not one big computer.

It is millions of computers, all connected together.

Kid version:

The internet is like a giant road system for messages.

Cars are messages. Roads are wires and Wi-Fi. Computers are houses and buildings.

How messages travel

When you click something, your computer sends a message like:

“Hello! Can I see that picture?”

Another computer answers:

“Sure! Here you go!”

This happens in tiny pieces, super fast.

Wires, waves, and magic (not really magic)

Messages travel using:

  • Wires under the ground
  • Cables under the sea
  • Wi-Fi through the air

Kid version:

Some messages drive through tunnels, some go over bridges, and some fly through the air like birds.

What lives on the internet?

Lots of things live on other people’s computers:

  • Websites
  • Videos
  • Games
  • Pictures
  • Messages

Your computer doesn’t keep all of these — it just visits them when you ask.

Try this: Be the internet

You need:

  • One “Sender”
  • One “Receiver”
  • One or more “Messengers”

The sender whispers a message to a messenger. The messenger runs it to the receiver. The receiver whispers back a reply.

That’s how the internet works — just much faster and with wires and Wi-Fi.

The big idea

The internet is:

  • Computers talking to computers
  • Using wires, cables, and Wi-Fi
  • Sending messages very, very fast

Next time, we’ll learn about something that lives on the internet called… The Cloud.
